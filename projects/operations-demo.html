Demo · Synthetic data

Operational Analytics

A single executive view of daily operations — throughput, labor utilization, and SLA adherence — so leaders can see the state of the operation at a glance.

Data & privacy

Originally built on a live operational database; rebuilt for this portfolio on fully synthetic data. Column names are preserved so the visuals and DAX rebind unchanged, but every value is regenerated — no real, nudged, or proprietary figures appear.

The challenge

A distribution operation moves through thousands of picks, packs, and trips a day, and the signals that actually matter — throughput keeping pace, labor matched to demand, service levels holding — are spread across systems and shift reports, not visible in one place. The hard part isn’t the data; it’s distilling it into the few things an operations leader can act on in the moment.

What it enables

Rather than a wall of metrics, the report is built around the questions an operations leader actually asks:

  • Are we keeping pace? — outbound throughput by hour and by facility
  • Is labor matched to the work? — scheduled vs. temp hours against actual throughput
  • Where is productivity slipping? — pick and checkout output per active hour
  • What’s the downstream load? — trip-building volume and bags per hour

How it’s built

Data model

Star schema: an operations fact table surrounded by date, shift, department, and location dimensions — kept deliberately simple, so each KPI traces back to a single grain.

DAX patterns

Throughput per hour, utilization ratios, and SLA-adherence percentages, plus period-over-period comparisons handled with time-intelligence measures.

Refresh

Re-pointed from the production database to a synthetic Excel source, then a full refresh with confirmation that no original rows remain. Scheduled refresh keeps the demo current.
