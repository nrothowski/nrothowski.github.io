Logistics & Delivery Analytics

An operational view of a grocery-delivery operation: are orders arriving on time, where is route capacity strained, and which exceptions need attention today?

Data & privacy

Originally built on a live operational database; rebuilt for this portfolio on fully synthetic data. Column names are preserved so the visuals and DAX rebind unchanged, but every value is regenerated — no real, nudged, or proprietary figures appear.

The challenge

A grocery-delivery operation runs hundreds of orders a day across dozens of routes, and the signals that actually matter — late deliveries, strained routes, exceptions that need a call — are scattered across transactional records, not visible in one place. The hard part isn’t the data; it’s distilling it into the few things a dispatcher or operations lead can act on in the moment.

What it enables

Rather than a wall of metrics, the report is built around the questions an operations lead actually asks:

  • Are we on track today? — on-time rate against target, at a glance
  • Where is demand concentrated? — order distribution across the service area
  • Which routes are strained? — route load and capacity pressure points
  • What needs attention now? — delivery exceptions surfaced, not buried

How it’s built

Data model

Star schema: a deliveries fact table surrounded by date, region/zone, route, and customer-segment dimensions — kept deliberately simple, so a new question rarely means a new model.

DAX patterns

On-time percentage, rolling 7-day averages, target-vs-actual variance, and exception counts — written as reusable measures rather than per-visual calculations.

Refresh

Re-pointed from the production database to a synthetic Excel source, then a full refresh with confirmation that no original rows remain. Scheduled refresh keeps the demo current.
